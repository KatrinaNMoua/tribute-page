Yuzuru Hanyu

Elegance in motion, strength in spirit— a skating legend whose artistry transcends the ice.

Timeline

1994 - Yuzuru Hanyu was born on December 7, 1994, in Sendai, Japan.
2004 - Hanyu began competing internationally at a young age, winning his first major competition, the 2004 Japan Novice Championships, at just 9 years old.
2010 - Hanyu competed at the World Junior Championships and finished 4th, showing his potential on the global stage.
2012 - Hanyu won his first Japanese Senior National Championship title, which launched his career into the elite level.
2014 - At the Sochi 2014 Winter Olympics, Hanyu won the gold medal in men's figure skating, making him the first Asian male figure skater to win an Olympic gold in this event.
2014 - Later in 2014, Hanyu won the World Figure Skating Championships in Saitama, Japan, cementing his status as a global champion.
2018 - At the PyeongChang 2018 Winter Olympics, Hanyu defended his Olympic title and won his second consecutive gold medal in men's figure skating.
2019 - Hanyu won the World Figure Skating Championships in Sapporo, Japan, further solidifying his place as one of the greatest figure skaters of all time.
2021 - Hanyu won the silver medal at the 2021 World Championships, continuing to perform at a high level despite injuries.
2022 - In 2022, Yuzuru Hanyu announced his decision to transition away from competitive skating, though he expressed his passion for the sport would continue in exhibitions and performances.
